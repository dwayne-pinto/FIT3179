From smog hanging over cities to smoke inside the home, air pollution poses a major threat to health across the globe. Almost all of the global population (99%) are exposed to air pollution levels that put them at increased risk for diseases including heart disease, stroke, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, cancer and pneumonia.
DALYs represents the number of years of good health lost to symptoms and damages of air pollution. Good health is impacted by various diseases bought about by the primary component of air pollution, PM2.5.
PM2.5 represents the concentration of small particles (0.000025mm) present in smoke. These irrate the respitory system leading to various respitory illnesses.

This unaceptable burden of disease is mostly endured by the developing world. With a lack of access to modern renewables countries combined with poorer health care, countries in Asia, Africa and the Mediterranean posses the highest death tolls.
